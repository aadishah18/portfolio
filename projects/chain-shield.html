USC Racing – Chain Shield

USC Racing (SAE) · Mechanical Engineering

I was involved in the design and integration of a drivetrain chain shield for the USC Racing vehicle. The component was developed to protect rotating drivetrain elements while maintaining proper chain clearance, alignment, and serviceability.

My work focused on mechanical geometry, mounting constraints, and integration with surrounding drivetrain components. The final part is not shown here due to team and competition restrictions.

USC Racing vehicle context showing drivetrain packaging and integration constraints.

Engineering Focus

  • Chain clearance under dynamic motion
  • Mounting geometry and tolerance stack-up
  • Integration with adjacent drivetrain components
  • Safety considerations and competition compliance
