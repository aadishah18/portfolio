Autonomous Vehicle Design

"King of the Hill" · Spring 2024

This project involved designing and building a small autonomous vehicle for a hill-climbing challenge where performance depended on torque, traction, and mechanical reliability rather than advanced autonomy.

I focused primarily on mechanical system design and integration — ensuring the chassis, drivetrain, and component placement could survive repeated runs and perform consistently under dynamic loading.

Full vehicle CAD assembly used to evaluate packaging, drivetrain layout, and subsystem integration.

Design Focus

  • Chassis stiffness and reinforcement under high torque loads
  • Drivetrain alignment to reduce mechanical losses
  • Component placement for stability, accessibility, and wiring robustness
  • Iterative debugging through physical testing
